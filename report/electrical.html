1. Overview of the Electrical System

The electrical subsystem delivers and regulates power throughout Jellybot, ensuring stable and efficient energy distribution to all components. It integrates the battery system, voltage regulators, and power management circuits to support long-duration underwater operation. Safety features such as overcurrent protection and waterproof connectors enhance reliability under varying load conditions.

3. Wiring Diagram

The electrical system consists of two air pumps, two solenoid valves, an Arduino microcontroller, and two motor drivers. The motor drivers are used to control both the pumps and solenoids, enabling precise timing and coordination of airflow. The solenoid valves regulate the direction of air—either channeling it into the silicone tentacles for inflation or releasing it into the atmosphere for deflation. This setup allows the Arduino to manage the inflation-deflation cycle automatically, ensuring consistent pressure control and repeatable actuation behavior during testing.

4.1 Pressure Testing for Optimal Actuation (1 Pump vs 2 Pump)

A series of controlled experiments were conducted to determine the optimal internal pressure of the pneumatic actuators for maximum expansion and responsiveness. Each configuration combined different numbers of DC air pumps and solenoid valves at varying supply voltages. The pressure values were measured using a digital barometric sensor connected to the actuator chamber. Results indicate that increasing voltage and the number of active pumps significantly improves internal pressure generation, enhancing actuator performance.

4.3 Bending Angle of Actuators (1 Pump vs 2 Pump)

The comparison scatter plot reveals a non-linear relationship between the internal pressure of the tentacles and their bending angle. At low pressures, the tentacle resists deformation, but once a certain threshold is reached, it bends rapidly before saturating at a maximum angle. This behavior indicates that while pressure primarily governs stiffness, the flow rate controls the actuation speed. Furthermore, increasing the number of pumps does not directly double the pressure, as pneumatic pressure is not additive. The material compliance of the silicone also limits further bending beyond a certain pressure, with thicker silicone walls resulting in smaller bend angles due to higher stiffness. However, several shortcomings were identified, including the manometer’s inability to capture gradual pressure changes, limited control over inflation and deflation rates, and the tendency for excessive inflation to cause ballooning. Moving forward, improvements will focus on using independent pump channels for each tentacle, experimenting with different silicone geometries and wall thicknesses, and testing the overall displacement of the JellyBot corresponding to each bend angle to better understand its propulsion efficiency.

5. Summary and Future Improvements

The electrical subsystem provides a stable and efficient foundation for Jellybot’s operation, ensuring consistent power delivery across all modules. Its design emphasizes reliability and modularity, enabling seamless integration with other subsystems. Future improvements could include implementing smart power monitoring systems, upgrading to higher energy-density batteries, and adopting wireless charging methods to improve efficiency and maintenance accessibility.
