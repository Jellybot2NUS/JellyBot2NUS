Overview

During Phase I of the JellyBot project, JellyBot 1.0 demonstrated its ability to remain neutrally buoyant under water and also displayed potential in its upwards propulsion as per the critical functions, Buoyancy and Propulsion respectively. However, from these testings, we discovered certain shortcomings which we would rectify and overcome in Phase II.

Phase II focuses on the practicality of our project which is the ability for underwater surveillance. To achieve this, 4 properties of JellyBot 2.0 must be verified:

  • 1) JellyBot 2.0 must descend to desired depths without manual assistance (Achieved through its Negative Buoyancy property)
  • 2) JellyBot 2.0 is able to achieve upward propulsion
  • 3) JellyBot 2.0 is able to achieve depth control for the purpose of capturing visual data
  • 4) JellyBot 2.0 is able to propel horizontally

As such, 4 different types of tests were conducted, to verify the above 4 properties.

Achieving Negative Buoyancy

Negative Buoyancy is achieved when the downward force also known as Weight is greater than the upward force also known as Buoyant Force. W > Fb

Mass of JellyBot 2.0

1) Mass of Battery pack with mounting devices, Flight Navigator and Camera = 530g

2) Mass of Voltage step down device with 2x Solenoid Valve = 57.5g

3) Mass of 2x Pneumatic Pump, 2x Motor Driver Board, I2C Driver = 217g

4) Mass of Base with 5m tether cable = 900.5g - 160g (Brass weights) = 740.5g

5) Mass of Cylinder body with acrylic dome = 677.5g

6) Mass of 2x Flap Clamp = 2 x 59.5g = 119g

7) Mass of Flap with 8x Tentacles = 503.5g

8) Mass of Weights = [2 x 50g] + [4 x (20g + 10g)] = 220g

Total mass = 530g + 57.5g + 217g + 740.5g + 677.5g + 119g + 503.5g + 220g = 3065g (3.065kg)

Weight (W) = mg = 3.065 x 9.81 ≈ 30.07N

Volume of water displaced by JellyBot 2.0

1) Volume of flap (annulus) ≈ 187.09cm³

2) Volume of 8x Tentacles = 304cm³

3) Volume of Housing ≈ 2349.51cm³

4) Volume of Dome ≈ 217.45cm³

5) Volume of 2x Flap Bracket ≈ 95.97cm³

6) Volume of exposed screws (M10, M14, Connectors, O2 Sensor) ≈ 40.48cm³

Total Displaced Volume ≈ 3194.5cm³

Buoyant Force (Fb) = p x g x v ≈ 31.34N

Result: JellyBot 2.0 is naturally Positively Buoyant as W < Fb (30.07N < 31.34N)

Additional Mass Required for Negative Buoyancy: To sink, an additional ~130g is theoretically needed for neutral buoyancy. Testing identified 400g of added mass as the optimal balance for sinking (3.33cm/s) while remaining light enough to propel upwards.

Propulsion Tests (Vertical Movement)

Findings: The prototype demonstrated repeatable thrust. Horizontal drift (6-11cm) suggests minor imbalances in mass distribution or asymmetric thrust.

Depth Control Tests

Conducted at NUS Swimming Pool (2m depth). The target was to maintain depth within ±5cm error.

Test Summary:

  • Test 1: Hovered between 50cm - 55cm. (Pass)
  • Test 2: Hovered between 53cm - 57cm. (Pass)
  • Test 4: Hovered between 48cm - 56cm. (Pass)

JellyBot 2.0 successfully remained within the specified error tolerances during manual depth control sessions.
