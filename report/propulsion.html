Calculations

To achieve neutral buoyancy, the total mass (Mtotal) must be equal to the total volume of water displaced (Vdisp,total). 

Mtotal = Vdisp,total
19.076Htotal + 1160.43 = 102.07Htotal + 737.33

-737.33 + 1160.43 = (102.07 - 19.076)Htotal
423.1 = 82.994Htotal

Htotal = 423.1 / 82.994
Htotal ≈ 5.10 cm

However, it is not possible for the height to be 5.10 cm due to the internal components and structural compatibility. Hence, to maintain neutral buoyancy, we must increase the mass required. 

Mneeded = [82.994Htotal - 423.1] g

For our Jellybot of Htotal = 15 cm:
Mneeded = [82.994(15) - 423.1] g
Mneeded ≈ 821.81 g

3. Buoyancy Tests

Neutral buoyancy is achieved when the upward force known as Buoyant Force = downward force also known as Weight as shown below.

Objective :

To verify that the Jellybot housing (i) maintains an upright (vertical) attitude in water and (ii) achieves neutral or slightly negative buoyancy so it remains submerged at a controlled depth rather than floating at the surface. This verification is a pre-requisite before open water trials to avoid uncontrolled floating and to confirm a stable trim suitable for imaging and sensing.

3.1 Procedure

  1. Remove tentacles and flaps.
  2. Wrap housing with two plastic layers and remove as much trapped air as possible.
  3. Place the chosen ballast at the base of the housing to lower its centre of gravity (CG).
  4. Lower the housing into the water tank, release, and allow it to settle.
  5. Record down observations:
    • Attitude (Uprightness)
    • Submergence distance from surface of water to top end-cap
    • Any other behaviour
  6. Repeat experiment as needed.

3.2 Acceptance Criteria

Buoyancy: Neutral / Slightly Negative — The housing remains submerged without rising to the surface.

Stability: The housing settles upright and stays that way; no tendency to roll.

3.3 Results and Observations
